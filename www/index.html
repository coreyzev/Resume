About

My name is Corey Zev Holland and I'm most often a front-end developer and a graphic designer. I'm currently working at a marketing agency developing tools to help people make the most important decisions they can make. It's an excellent opportunity where I'm enabled to find creative solutions to creative and technical problems.

I graduated from Northeastern University with a design degree, but a lot of my time recently has been spent developing my skill with code. I spent a discrete chunk of time learning JavaScript online and I've been writing it as much as possible. I'd love to find a position where I can marry my passions effectively. I'm looking for front-end positions where I can make this happen.

I'd previously always worked as an internal designer/dev for non-creative companies so far, so I was hoping to find a company where I can thrive. I was able to start that process here, working with various clients and many different teams. Now I'm looking for an opportunity to grow my experience.

I look forward to hearing from you soon. Of course, I am taking my time choosing the best opportunity for myself.

Thank you for taking the time to read this.

What's shaped me?

Since receiving a computer in grade school I have constantly been consuming content. While I spent hours playing games, using the encyclopedia, surfing the web, and chatting with people. I was unwittingly absorbing these interactions as fuel for my future. Those experiences have made user interaction design almost a second nature to me.

Adjectives to describe me:

Nerdy, techy, determined,
active, & unique.

Who inspires me?

An odd collection of designers feed my creativity. I like the control which the swiss designers demonstrate. Focusing on the typography as the strength of their designs. Though designer Chip Kidd inspires me as well, breaking the grid when needed. His approch to design is similar to a scientist's. It's like solving a problem.
